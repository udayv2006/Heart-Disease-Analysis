About the condition

Heart disease is a group of conditions affecting the cardiovascular system, arising from damage to the epicardium, pericardium, myocardium, endocardium, valves, or vessels of the heart.

According to the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute's Framingham study, the most significant contributors to cardiovascular disease are obesity, a sedentary lifestyle, and smoking — each of which is explored in the dashboards linked below.

Explore the data →

Obesity

Excess body weight increases strain on the heart and raises blood pressure and cholesterol.

Sedentary lifestyle

Low physical activity is linked to weaker cardiovascular function over time.

Smoking

Tobacco use damages blood vessels and significantly raises cardiovascular risk.
